Eye Tracking

Sentence explaining the significance of this test.

Horizontal

Watch the eyes while moving eyeball at a foot in front of him/her. Use a beanbag if child moves his/her head. Bumpy-eyes jiggle. Unable-eyes do not follow the eyeball.

Needed bean bag on head

Vertical

Watch the eyes while moving the eyeball up and down in front of him/her.

Skipping

Sentence explaining the significance of this test.


Have the child skip to the end of the hall - 20 feet and come back. If not sure, repeat this step.

Teaming with Pencil Eraser

Sentence explaining the significance of this test.


Hold a pencil with an eraser on top and a small dot marked on it in front of the child. Tell the child to look at the dot. Observe if the eyes cross or slide off.

Visual Discrimination

Sentence explaining the significance of this test.


Hold a sheet and ask the child to find similar pictures in this row as the first picture. Similarly, ask the child to find picture not similar to the first picture.

Tummy Crawl

Sentence explaining the significance of this test.


Ask the child to get down on the tummy and crawl from one end to other and get back the same way. Observe how he/she uses legs and arms.
